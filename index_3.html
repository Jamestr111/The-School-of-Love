Our Soul

Designed for one —
and one alone

The School of Love was born from a recognition that the most sacred work a woman can do is the work she does alone — with herself, inside herself, for herself.

While the world rushes toward the collective, we built a sanctuary for the singular. No groups. No strangers. No noise. Just 1,000 square feet of intentional space in Doral, Florida, held entirely for you.

Like the Brahma Kumaris teach — the soul is a star of light residing within the self. Every room in this sanctuary was designed to help that star remember its own luminosity.

What Awaits You

Four rooms.
One sacred purpose.

Each space holds a different quality of silence. Each is reset with fresh flowers, cleared air, and intentional scent. You will never enter a room that wasn't prepared entirely for you.

Room I

The Lotus Room

The jewel of the sanctuary. Natural light filters through linen panels onto floor cushions in ivory and sage. Himalayan salt lamps line the baseboards in warm amber glow. A gentle clay incense moves slowly through the air. This is where you sit with what matters most.

Room II

The Sound Bath Studio

Lie beneath hand-tuned crystal bowls in a room built entirely for resonance. Every solo session is a private vibrational experience — the sound meets you where language cannot. Your cells remember what your mind has forgotten.

Room III

The Breath & Light Lounge

An audio-guided breathwork sanctuary with soft LED cove lighting that shifts with your practice. No instructor needed. The room itself holds the arc — from release, through expansion, into stillness. You leave lighter than you arrived.

The Path

The Immersive Solo Retreat

A curated half-day journey moving through all spaces, guided by a personal audio arc created for you. Begins in Breath, moves into Sound, rests in the Lotus Room, and closes at the First Edition Shelf with a gemstone ritual kit to carry home. For the serious seeker.

The Space

A silence engineered by love

In CityPlace or Downtown Doral — 1,000 sq ft where every architectural and sensory decision was made to defeat noise outside and amplify the voice within.

Acoustic Architecture

Dual-pane laminated glass seals out the flight path. Heavy acoustic felt panels — designed as wall art — absorb every whisper of outside noise.

Himalayan Salt Lamps

Hand-carved salt lamps glow along every baseboard — warm amber that cleanses the air, grounds the body, and cradles the nervous system into ease.

Muted Terracotta

The signature wall color is a Root Chakra decision. Muted terracotta grounds the nervous system so the high-vibration work of inner transformation can begin.

Tactile Luxury

Layered jute-and-shag rugs, ivory and sage meditation cushions placed with ceremony. To sit here is to be held by every material that surrounds you.

Who This Is For

For the woman who has done the outer work and knows the inner work is next. For the seeker who is tired of performing peace and ready to simply inhabit it. For her — and her alone.

The 12-Month Path

The Path to Wholeness

A classic initiation structure — four quarters, four awakenings. Each phase deepens what the last revealed. This is not a program. It is a remembering.

I
Q1 · Months 1–3
Worthiness

The most essential foundation. In a world that equates value with output, we begin by unlearning. The Sacred Receiving module teaches what no business school does: how to breathe before you build.

Sacred Receiving
II
Q2 · Months 4–6
Clarity

With worthiness rooted, the mind grows still enough to see. This quarter cultivates discernment — the capacity to hear the difference between fear and intuition, ambition and calling.

Inner Vision
III
Q3 · Months 7–9
Abundance

High-vibration work. Aligning the energetic body with the reality you are calling in — wealth, health, love, creative power. Practical spirituality at its fullest expression.

Prosperity Consciousness
IV
Q4 · Months 10–12
Legacy

The sacred closed loop. Those who have received begin to give — contributing back to the very structures that held them. Wholeness expressed not as achievement, but as service.

Sacred Giving
